# Residency — privacy policy

_Last updated: 6 July 2026_

Residency counts the days you spend in each country to help you track tax-residency
thresholds. It is designed so that your location history never leaves your device.

## What the app collects

**Location (on your device only).** With your permission, the app reads your device's
location in the background to determine which **country** you are in. Raw coordinates
are processed on your device and stored only in the app's local database on your phone.
**Precise location, coordinates, movement history, and timestamps of fixes are never
transmitted to us or to any third party.** Country lookup happens entirely offline
against map data bundled with the app — no location data is sent to a geocoding service.

**Country-level day counts (optional sync).** If you create an account and enable sync,
the app uploads only derived, country-level records of the form "on 2026-03-04 a day was
counted in France" — no coordinates, no times of day, no accuracy data. This is the
minimum needed to back up your day counts and restore them on a new device. Without an
account, nothing is uploaded at all and the app is fully functional offline.

**Account data (optional).** If you sign in, we store your email address for
authentication. Nothing else.

## What the app does not do

- No advertising, no trackers, no analytics SDKs.
- No sale or sharing of data with third parties.
- No use of your data for any purpose other than showing you your own day counts.
- No collection of contacts, photos, browsing data, or identifiers for tracking.

## Notifications

Threshold warnings are generated **on your device** by comparing your local day counts
to the residency rules you configure. No push-notification server is involved.

## Data retention and deletion

All location-derived data lives in the app's local database; deleting the app deletes
it. If you used sync, you can delete your synced day counts and account at any time from
within the app or by contacting us, and we will remove them from our systems.

## Your choices

- Location permission is optional; the app explains its use before asking and remains
  usable with reduced functionality if you decline background access.
- Sync is opt-in and off by default.

## Important note

Residency is a record-keeping tool, not tax advice. Day counts — especially days
inferred during tracking gaps, which the app flags — should be reviewed with a qualified
tax advisor before being relied upon.
